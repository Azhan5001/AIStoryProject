# Web Design Direction

Design inspirations and guidelines for the AI.R Taletoreum frontend.
Both teams are free to choose their own specific fonts, colours, and layout, this document provides direction, not a strict template.

## Typography

- Headings/Titles: Sans-Serif (e.g., Inter, Poppins, Nunito)
- Body/Paragraph: Serif (e.g., Lora, Merriweather, Source Serif Pro)
- Minimum 16px body text
- Both teams choose their own specific fonts. These are just examples

## Website Inspirations

- **Anthropic** (anthropic.com) — clean layout, generous whitespace, modern but warm
- **Duolingo** (duolingo.com) — friendly, colourful without being overwhelming, playful illustrations
- **Epic!** (getepic.com) — kids' digital reading platform, colourful, story-focused cards, friendly illustrations
- **Storybird** (storybird.com) — storytelling platform, visual-first, simple navigation
- **Canva** (canva.com) — clean dashboard, card-based layout, intuitive creation flow

## Design Principles

- Playful and inviting, with professional polish (children enjoy it, parents trust it — think Pixar, not clip-art)
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Generous whitespace
- Warm, inviting colours (soft pastels or earth tones)
- WCAG AA contrast minimum
- Simple navigation — 3 clicks max to start creating
- Loading states with friendly messages (the AI takes a moment to generate stories)

## What NOT to Do

- Comic Sans or overly "kiddy" fonts
- Neon/bright primary colour overload
- Cluttered layouts
- Too many options on one screen
